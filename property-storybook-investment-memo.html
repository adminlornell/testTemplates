Risk Factors

Key risks to consider for this investment

  • Building Age & Deferred Maintenance

    Built in 1900, the property may require capital expenditures for building systems, roof, HVAC, and other infrastructure. Conduct thorough property condition assessment during due diligence.

  • Lease-Up Risk

    The 3,495 SF of vacant space must be leased to achieve stabilized returns. Market absorption may take 6-12 months depending on tenant demand and competition.

  • Tenant Concentration

    Two major tenants account for 54% of occupied space. Loss of either tenant would significantly impact cash flow until replacement tenant is secured.

  • Market Risk

    General economic downturn or changes in healthcare industry dynamics could affect tenant demand for medical office space in Worcester market.

  • Rent Growth Assumptions

    Projected rent increases assume continued strong healthcare sector growth in Worcester. Market conditions may vary from projections.

  • Capital Requirements

    Value-add strategy requires capital for tenant improvements, leasing commissions, and potential building improvements. Budget accordingly with reserves.

Risk Mitigation Strategies

  • Conduct Phase I environmental and comprehensive property condition assessment
  • Maintain CapEx reserve of $50,000+ for unexpected repairs
  • Begin marketing vacant space immediately upon acquisition
  • Engage experienced property management team familiar with medical office operations
  • Structure conservative financing with sufficient cash flow coverage
  • Build relationships with existing tenants to encourage lease renewals
